# Source Adapters

Use this file when deciding how to gather and interpret audience signals from different public surfaces.

## Official Brand And Product Pages

- Treat these as primary evidence for intended audience, positioning, and motivation cues.
- Record the language the brand chooses: what promise is emphasized, what use case is centered, and what type of buyer is implicitly addressed.
- Do not assume the brand's intended audience is the same as its actual audience response.

## Collection Pages And Navigation

- Use these for assortment logic, price ladder, and how the brand segments buyers.
- A brand with clear category splits or filter language may reveal distinct audience clusters.
- Navigation is often a better audience clue than marketing headlines alone.

## Reviews And FAQs

- Use these for observed audience signals: who is actually buying, what they care about, and what they worry about.
- Review language often surfaces buyer maturity, urgency, and tolerance for tradeoffs.
- FAQs often reveal what uncertain buyers need before converting.

## Reddit And Community Threads

- Use for language richness, objection patterns, and expert-versus-beginner differences.
- Treat as noisier than primary surfaces.
- Prefer firsthand use, comparison, or troubleshooting discussions over generic recommendation lists.

## TikTok, YouTube, And Public Social Surfaces

- Use when creator framing, comments, or visible reactions reveal who the product resonates with.
- Treat social as supporting evidence, not the sole basis for audience claims.
- Watch for differences between creator-facing appeal and buyer-facing appeal.

## Competitor Pages

- Use when audience contrast is hard to infer in isolation.
- Competitors can clarify whether the focal target is premium, beginner-friendly, gift-led, or utility-led relative to the market.
- Do not turn the task into a full competitor teardown unless the user asks for it.

## Unknown Or Weak Sources

- Downgrade evidence quality when the source is scraped, snippet-only, or weakly attributable.
- If audience inference depends on a thin source, mark it as a hypothesis rather than an observed pattern.
